Golden Booth Brawl

Build It. Brand It.

Booth Rules & Guidelines

  • Each booth may sell products and/or services (can be multiple)
  • All offerings must be safe, school-appropriate, and hygienic
  • Booth must display: booth name & theme, price list, and a simple expense/sales tracking sheet
  • No budget cap — teams are encouraged to keep costs reasonable
  • Profit = Total Earnings − Expenses

Setup & Environment

  • Venue: Covered court, multipurpose hall, or open campus space
  • Booth Area: Zoned by level (Elementary / JHS / SHS), then assigned per section within each zone
  • Sections may prepare and pre-assemble booth materials the day before the event

Event Flow

Step 1: Opening & Ad/Sales Talk Challenge
Each booth delivers a 2-minute advertisement/pitch on stage (per program). Sales officially begin immediately after.
Step 2: Selling Period
Booths operate and sell to visitors. Judges conduct visits and scoring during this period.
Step 3: Closing & Accounting (15–20 min)
Teams count cash, finalize sales totals, compute profit, and submit finance summary. The booth contest runs for 2 days. Final total sales counting (Day 1 + Day 2) must be submitted not later than 2:00 PM on Day 2.
Step 4: Awards & Recognition (10–15 min)
Announce winners per category and recognize participation.

Required Finance Documents

  • Itemized expense list (with receipts if required)
  • Price list
  • Total sales computation
Note: Booths are organized into 3 zones only: Elementary, Junior High, and Senior High. Setup and flow arranged per program level to fit the venue layout.

Fix-It Fest

Small hands. Golden solutions.

Team Formation

  • Each team consists of 5 members
  • Grades 1–3: 4 students + 1 parent
  • Grades 4–6: 5 students
  • One team represents each section
  • Team selection should be completed at least 2 weeks before the contest

Setup & Environment

  • Seating: Teams sit together at designated tables, quiz bee style
  • Materials provided: Drawing paper or whiteboard, markers/colored pencils, writing materials
  • Timer visible to all participants
  • GRASPS Prompt Card/Slide displayed for every problem

Round Structure (3 Rounds)

Step 1: GRASPS Prompt Presentation (1 minute)
Game Master presents a GRASPS-formatted problem covering Goal, Role, Audience, Situation, Product/Performance, and Standards. Prompt is displayed visually and read aloud. Teams may ask clarifying questions (30 seconds).
Step 2: Solution Development (3 minutes)
Teams brainstorm and design within constraints. Must produce a labeled sketch (required) and a short pitch outline (recommended): Problem → Solution → How it works. When the timer ends, all work must stop immediately.
Step 3: Presentation Order Randomization (1 minute)
Random selection determines presentation order (drawing numbers or digital randomizer).
Step 4: Team Presentations (2 minutes per team)
Presenters follow the GRASPS Pitch Format in this fixed order:
  1. Role: "We are the ___."
  2. Goal: "Our goal is to ___."
  3. Audience: "This helps ___ because ___."
  4. Situation/Constraints: "We must ___ (constraints)."
  5. Product/Performance: "Our solution is ___." + show sketch
  6. How it works: Step → Step → Step
  7. Success link: "We will succeed because ___ (tie to standards)."
Step 5: Judging & Scoring
Judges score using the GRASPS-aligned rubric. Scores recorded but not announced until the end.

Winner Determination

  • Champion and 2 Runner-Ups per bracket
  • Highest cumulative score across all 3 rounds
  • Tiebreaker: judges deliberate or use bonus challenge

DCCian InnoPitch

The Golden Breakthrough

Team Composition

  • 10 learners per team (maximum)
  • One team represents each section in the final Pitch Proper
  • Finalists: 6 JHS + 6 SHS teams

Selection Process (3-Week Structure)

Week 1: Recorded Lecture Series (1 hour total, modular)
  • Introduction to techno + team formation
  • Ideation process + customer discovery
  • Pitch deck composition + pitching basics
Week 2: Section-Level Selection
Teams create and submit a draft pitch deck (slides) and optional 1-page concept summary. Advisers select the best team using the selection rubric.
Week 3: Pitch Proper
Finalist teams pitch on Demo Day.

Pitch Deck Requirements

  • 6–10 slides, max 5 minutes pitch time
  • Must include: Problem, Target users & context, Solution & key features, Why it is different, Feasibility & execution plan, Simple costing or sustainability plan, Impact (school/community relevance)

Demo Day Flow

Step 1: Registration & Opening (15–30 minutes)
Welcome remarks, brief orientation for judges and audience.
Step 2: Pitch Presentations
Each team presents within 5 minutes max. Judges score in real time.
Step 3: Score Tabulation + Awards
Judges submit final scores. Tiebreakers: Feasibility & Execution Plan → Pitch Delivery & Teamwork → Judges' deliberation.
Note: Judges should avoid penalizing teams for limited resources — focus on reasoning and clarity.

InfoGold

Visualizing Innovation

Infographic Requirements

Must include:

  • Project Title
  • Problem Statement (1–2 sentences)
  • Target Users
  • Proposed Solution (how it works)
  • Key Benefits / Impact
  • Feasibility (resources needed and next steps)

Optional sections:

  • Customer discovery highlights (quotes or counts)
  • Simple cost estimate or budget
  • Implementation timeline (3–5 steps)

Format Specifications

  • Size: A3 or 20×30 inches
  • Orientation: Portrait
  • Include team name and section/grade
  • Use large, readable text from 2–3 meters away
  • 4 entries per section

Gallery Walk Flow

Step 1: Setup (30–45 minutes)
Teams arrange posters and basic booth design. Optional 1-minute rehearsal of poster explanation.
Step 2: Gallery Walk
Visitors circulate and view posters. Teams stay at booths to explain when asked.
Step 3: Judging / Voting (optional)
Judges score using the rubric. Optional: People's Choice voting.
Step 4: Closing & Recognition
Announce optional awards and recognize participation.
Quality rule: Clear and readable is better than decorative. Display options include poster board on tables, or mounted board on easels or wall.
