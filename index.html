Detailed Program Flow

Two action-packed days of innovation, entrepreneurship, and creative problem-solving.

8:00 AM
Registration & Assembly
All participants, faculty, and staff assemble at the main venue. Attendance check and area orientation.
8:30 AM
Day 1 Opening Ceremony
  • Opening Remarks
  • Special Message
  • Statement of the Purpose
9:00 AM
Booth Opening Ceremony
  • Booth contest mechanics overview
  • Presentation of Judges
  • Sales officially open after this segment
9:30 AM
Ad & Sales Talk Segment
Each booth (per program) delivers a 2-minute advertisement/pitch on stage. Ethical marketing reminders apply.
10:00 AM – 12:00 PM
Fix-It Fest: Grades 1–3 MORNING RUN
6 teams compete in 3 GRASPS-aligned problem-solving rounds. Each round: 1-min prompt, 3-min brainstorm, 2-min presentations, scoring.
1:00 PM – 3:00 PM
Fix-It Fest: Grades 4–6 AFTERNOON RUN
5 teams compete in 3 GRASPS-aligned problem-solving rounds. Same format as morning session.
3:00 PM – 5:00 PM
Booth Judging Block (All Year Levels)
Parallel judging streams:
  • InfoGold: Visualizing Innovation — Gallery walk & judging
  • Golden Booth Brawl: Most Innovative Booth Design + Best Innovative Product/Service
8:00 AM
Registration & Assembly
Day 2 attendance, recap, and setup verification for booths and pitch areas.
8:30 AM
Day 2 Opening Ceremony
Welcome back, brief recap of Day 1 highlights, and orientation for Day 2 activities.
10:00 AM – 12:00 PM
DCCian InnoPitch: JHS Finals MORNING
6 Junior High School finalist teams pitch their innovations. 5 minutes max per team + Q&A.
1:00 PM – 3:00 PM
DCCian InnoPitch: SHS Finals AFTERNOON
6 Senior High School finalist teams pitch their innovations. 5 minutes max per team + Q&A.
3:00 PM
Final Accounting & Submission Window
All booths finalize combined Day 1 + Day 2 sales/profit. Deadline: 4:00 PM. Finance summaries submitted to organizers.
3:30 PM
Awarding Ceremony
  • 1) Fix-It Fest Awards (Grades 1–6)
  • 2) Booth Awards (Golden Booth Brawl categories)
  • 3) InfoGold Awards
  • 4) Pitch Awards (InnoPitch JHS & SHS)
4:30 PM
Closing Ceremony
  • Closing remarks
  • Acknowledgements
  • Photo opportunity
5:00 PM
Egress
Booth cleanup, venue reset, documentation wrap-up.

Scheduling Optimization Notes

  • Fix-It Fest + Booth/InfoGold (Day 1): No time overlap. The Fix-It Fest panel finishes by 3:00 PM, and booth judging starts at 3:00 PM. The same judges can serve both if available.
  • InfoGold + Booth Brawl (Day 1 PM): These run in parallel. If using separate criteria, the same judges can evaluate both during booth visits. Otherwise, split into 2 sub-panels.
  • InnoPitch (Day 2): Completely independent of Day 1. Can recruit external professionals who are unavailable on Day 1.
  • Minimum scenario (7 judges): 5 judges serve all Day 1 events (Fix-It Fest + Booth + InfoGold); 5 of them return or 7 fresh judges serve Day 2 InnoPitch.
  • Recommended scenario (12–15 judges): 5 for Fix-It Fest, 5 for Booth+InfoGold (with 2–3 overlap from Fix-It panel), 5–7 for InnoPitch.

Grades 1–2 Problem Bank (10 Problems)

1
Clean Playground Patrol
G — Goal:Keep the playground clean and safe.
R — Role:Playground Helpers.
A — Audience:Students and teachers on duty.
S — Situation:No extra staff; must be easy and fun; works during recess.
P — Product:A tool or simple service (with labeled drawing) that helps kids throw trash properly.
S — Standards:Safe, easy to use, reduces litter, clear explanation.
2
Remember My Things
G — Goal:Help pupils remember to bring school materials.
R — Role:Class Routine Designers.
A — Audience:Grade 1–2 pupils and parents.
S — Situation:No phones required; works every school day; simple steps.
P — Product:A reminder system (chart/tool/service) with 3-step routine.
S — Standards:Kid-friendly, realistic at home/school, clear routine.
3
Orderly Lunch Line
G — Goal:Make lunchtime more organized.
R — Role:Lunch Line Leaders.
A — Audience:Pupils lining up and canteen staff.
S — Situation:Limited space; must avoid pushing; quick to follow.
P — Product:A method/tool (signs, floor marks, tokens, etc.) shown in a sketch.
S — Standards:Improves order, safe, easy to follow.
4
Quiet Classroom Reset
G — Goal:Help the class become quiet fast after activities.
R — Role:Classroom Calm Coaches.
A — Audience:Classmates and adviser.
S — Situation:Must work without shouting; no special equipment.
P — Product:A signal routine/tool with 3 steps.
S — Standards:Effective, respectful, simple.
5
Lost-and-Found Buddy
G — Goal:Help return lost items to owners.
R — Role:Lost-and-Found Helpers.
A — Audience:Pupils and teachers.
S — Situation:Items get mixed up; must be easy to check; privacy-friendly.
P — Product:A simple labeling or claiming system with a drawing.
S — Standards:Easy, fair, reduces lost items.
6
Handwashing Hero
G — Goal:Encourage proper handwashing.
R — Role:Health Helpers.
A — Audience:Pupils using the wash area.
S — Situation:Limited water/time; must be fun and fast.
P — Product:A reminder tool/game/chant system illustrated.
S — Standards:Promotes correct steps, practical, fun.
7
Kind Words Machine
G — Goal:Reduce teasing and increase kind words.
R — Role:Friendship Builders.
A — Audience:Classmates.
S — Situation:Must be simple; works daily; no one gets embarrassed.
P — Product:A system/tool that helps kids give kind messages.
S — Standards:Inclusive, safe, easy, improves kindness.
8
Pack-Up Fast
G — Goal:Help pupils pack up quickly and correctly.
R — Role:Pack-Up Trainers.
A — Audience:Pupils and adviser.
S — Situation:Only 5 minutes; must work for different bags.
P — Product:A checklist tool/system shown in a sketch.
S — Standards:Fast, clear, reduces forgotten items.
9
Sharing Station
G — Goal:Make sharing classroom materials fair.
R — Role:Sharing Managers.
A — Audience:Pupils during group work.
S — Situation:Limited supplies; must avoid fights.
P — Product:A borrowing/return system with tokens or roles.
S — Standards:Fair, simple, reduces conflict.
10
Safe Walk Signs
G — Goal:Help kids walk safely in hallways.
R — Role:Safety Sign Designers.
A — Audience:Pupils moving between rooms.
S — Situation:Must be easy to understand quickly; works without teacher reminders.
P — Product:Signage/path idea drawn and labeled.
S — Standards:Clear, safe, realistic.
